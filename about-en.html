I'm a Product Designer with roots in graphic and multimedia design, but my real work happens at the intersection of design, strategy, and technology. I've specialized in complex digital products — financial platforms, design systems, enterprise experiences — where I learned that designing well isn't just about clean interfaces: it's about building structures that teams can sustain and evolve over time.

A significant part of my career was spent working directly with executive leadership: conceptualizing products from scratch, prototyping ideas to validate them with real clients, and translating business opportunities into clear, actionable proposals. That context taught me to move fast, communicate precisely, and understand design as a tool that reduces uncertainty — not just makes things look better.

Today I'm especially interested in how design's role is changing in the face of AI and new ways of building products. Not as a threat, but as an opportunity to redefine what it means to add value as a designer. I believe the best products emerge when design sits at the same table as business and technology — and that's exactly where I like to be.
